# Groundnut Leaf Spot (Cercospora arachidicola)

## Overview
Groundnut (peanut) leaf spot is a fungal disease affecting groundnut plants, caused by *Cercospora arachidicola* (early leaf spot) and *Cercosporidium personatum* (late leaf spot). It's one of the most destructive groundnut diseases, causing significant yield loss through defoliation.

## Symptoms
### Early Leaf Spot
- Small, circular lesions on leaves (1-5 mm diameter)
- Lesions are brown with dark borders
- Yellow halo around lesions
- Lesions primarily on upper leaf surface
- Spots may coalesce in severe cases

### Late Leaf Spot
- Larger, dark brown to black lesions
- Lesions on both leaf surfaces
- No distinct yellow halo
- Lesions may have raised centers
- More severe defoliation than early leaf spot

## Conditions
- Early leaf spot: 25-30°C optimal
- Late leaf spot: 28-32°C optimal
- High humidity (>85%) and leaf wetness required
- Warm, wet weather promotes rapid spread
- Continuous groundnut cultivation increases risk
- Overhead irrigation spreads spores
- Spores survive in crop residue and soil

## Treatment
### Chemical Control
- Chlorothalonil: 2-3g/L, protective sprays every 7-10 days
- Propiconazole 25% EC: 0.5 mL/L
- Tebuconazole 25% EC: 0.5 mL/L
- Azoxystrobin: 1-1.5 mL/L, systemic action
- Mancozeb 75% WP: 2g/L for preventive sprays
- Carbendazim 50% WP: 1g/L for curative action

### Cultural Control
- Plant resistant varieties when available
- Rotate crops (2-3 years away from groundnut)
- Use certified disease-free seeds
- Improve air circulation through proper spacing
- Avoid overhead irrigation, use drip irrigation
- Remove infected plant debris
- Practice deep plowing to bury residue
- Maintain balanced fertilization

### Expected Recovery
- 7-14 days after chemical treatment if caught early
- 50-70% yield recovery if treated promptly
- Prevention more effective than cure
- Severe infections may cause permanent yield loss
- Early detection and treatment critical

## Cost Estimate
- Fungicide spray: ₹500-1000/acre
- Labor: ₹200-300/acre
- Total: ₹700-1300/acre per application
- Multiple applications (3-4) typically needed: ₹2100-5200/acre total

## Prevention Tips
- Start preventive sprays before flowering stage
- Monitor fields regularly, especially after rainy periods
- Use resistant groundnut varieties
- Maintain proper plant spacing for air circulation
- Avoid working with plants when wet
- Implement crop rotation practices
- Remove and destroy infected plant debris
- Ensure proper drainage in fields
