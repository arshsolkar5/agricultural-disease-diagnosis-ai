# Northern Corn Leaf Blight (Exserohilum turcicum)

## Overview
Northern corn leaf blight is a fungal disease affecting corn/maize plants, caused by *Exserohilum turcicum*. It's one of the most destructive corn diseases worldwide, causing significant yield loss through leaf damage and reduced photosynthesis.

## Symptoms
- Long, cigar-shaped lesions on leaves (1-15 cm long)
- Lesions are grayish-green to tan with distinct borders
- Lesions typically appear on lower leaves first
- In severe cases, lesions coalesce causing extensive leaf death
- Premature leaf death reduces grain fill and yield
- Stalk weakening and lodging in severe infections

## Conditions
- Temperature 18-27°C optimal for infection
- High humidity (>90%) and leaf wetness required
- Warm, wet weather promotes rapid spread
- Continuous corn cultivation increases risk
- Overhead irrigation spreads spores
- Spores survive in crop residue and soil
- Susceptible hybrids more prone to infection

## Treatment
### Chemical Control
- Azoxystrobin: 1-1.5 mL/L, preventive sprays
- Pyraclostrobin: 1-1.5 mL/L, systemic action
- Propiconazole 25% EC: 0.5 mL/L
- Tebuconazole 25% EC: 0.5 mL/L
- Mancozeb 75% WP: 2g/L for preventive sprays
- Chlorothalonil: 2-3g/L, protective fungicide

### Cultural Control
- Plant resistant hybrids when available
- Rotate crops (2-3 years away from corn)
- Till to incorporate crop residue
- Avoid overhead irrigation, use drip irrigation
- Maintain proper plant density for air circulation
- Balance nitrogen fertilization
- Remove infected plant debris
- Use certified disease-free seeds

### Expected Recovery
- 7-14 days after chemical treatment if caught early
- 50-70% yield recovery if treated promptly
- Prevention more effective than cure
- Severe infections may cause permanent yield loss
- Early detection and treatment critical

## Cost Estimate
- Fungicide spray: ₹600-1200/acre
- Labor: ₹200-300/acre
- Total: ₹800-1500/acre per application
- Multiple applications (2-3) typically needed: ₹1600-4500/acre total

## Prevention Tips
- Start preventive sprays before tasseling stage
- Monitor fields regularly, especially after rainy periods
- Use resistant corn varieties
- Maintain proper plant spacing for air circulation
- Avoid working with plants when wet
- Implement crop rotation practices
- Remove and destroy crop residue after harvest
- Ensure proper drainage in fields
