# Cotton Boll Rot (Various Fungi)

## Overview
Cotton boll rot is a complex disease affecting cotton bolls, caused by multiple fungi including *Fusarium*, *Alternaria*, *Aspergillus*, and *Rhizopus* species. It causes significant yield loss and fiber quality reduction, especially under humid conditions.

## Symptoms
- Water-soaked lesions on bolls
- Bolls turn brown to black
- Internal boll rot and lint discoloration
- Fungal growth on affected bolls (white, pink, or black)
- Premature boll opening
- Reduced lint quality and yield
- Hard, matted fibers in severe cases
- Sour or musty odor from affected bolls

## Conditions
- Temperature 25-35°C optimal for infection
- High humidity (>85%) and rainfall critical
- Warm, wet weather promotes rapid spread
- Poor air circulation in dense canopy
- Insect damage to bolls increases infection risk
- Excessive nitrogen fertilization
- Late-season rainfall during boll development
- Plant stress increases susceptibility

## Treatment
### Chemical Control
- Carbendazim 50% WP: 1g/L, spray every 10-12 days
- Propiconazole 25% EC: 0.5 mL/L
- Azoxystrobin: 1-1.5 mL/L, preventive sprays
- Mancozeb 75% WP: 2g/L for preventive applications
- Thiophanate-methyl: 1g/L for curative action
- Copper oxychloride: 3g/L for preventive sprays

### Cultural Control
- Plant resistant varieties when available
- Maintain proper plant spacing for air circulation
- Avoid excessive nitrogen fertilization
- Control insect pests that damage bolls
- Ensure proper drainage in fields
- Avoid late planting in high-risk areas
- Remove and destroy infected bolls
- Practice crop rotation (2-3 years)

### Expected Recovery
- 10-15 days post-treatment for new infections
- 40-60% yield recovery if caught early
- Prevention critical due to boll susceptibility
- Severe infections may cause permanent yield loss
- Fiber quality may be permanently affected

## Cost Estimate
- Fungicide spray: ₹600-1200/acre
- Labor: ₹200-300/acre
- Total: ₹800-1500/acre per application
- Multiple applications (2-3) typically needed: ₹1600-4500/acre total

## Prevention Tips
- Start preventive sprays at boll formation stage
- Monitor fields regularly during boll development
- Control insect pests that create entry points
- Avoid overhead irrigation during boll development
- Maintain balanced fertilization
- Ensure proper field drainage
- Harvest at proper maturity to reduce exposure
- Store cotton in dry, well-ventilated conditions
