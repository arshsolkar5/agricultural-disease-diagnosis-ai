# Sugarcane Red Rot (Colletotrichum falcatum)

## Overview
Sugarcane red rot is a fungal disease affecting sugarcane, caused by *Colletotrichum falcatum*. It's one of the most destructive sugarcane diseases, causing significant yield loss through stalk rotting and reduced juice quality.

## Symptoms
- Reddish internal tissue with white spots in stalks
- Sour alcoholic odor from affected stalks
- Stalks become hollow and collapse
- Leaf drying and wilting from top downward
- Red lesions on leaf sheaths
- Reduced cane weight and juice quality
- Stalks may break easily in affected areas
- Fungal growth in severe infections

## Conditions
- Temperature 25-32°C optimal for infection
- High humidity (>85%) and rainfall critical
- Waterlogged conditions promote disease
- Poor drainage increases risk
- Continuous sugarcane cultivation increases inoculum
- Infected setts (seed cane) spread disease
- Insect damage creates entry points
- Nutrient stress increases susceptibility

## Treatment
### Chemical Control
- Carbendazim 50% WP: 2g/L sett treatment
- Thiophanate-methyl: 2g/kg sett treatment
- Trichoderma viride: 10g/kg sett treatment
- Hot water treatment of setts (50°C for 30 min)
- Copper oxychloride: 3g/L for spray applications
- Mancozeb 75% WP: 2g/L for preventive sprays

### Cultural Control
- Use disease-free setts from healthy fields
- Hot water or fungicide treatment of setts
- Plant resistant varieties when available
- Improve field drainage
- Avoid waterlogging
- Remove and destroy infected stalks
- Practice crop rotation (2-3 years)
- Maintain balanced fertilization

### Expected Recovery
- Limited recovery once infection established
- Prevention critical - no effective cure
- Early detection allows removal of affected stools
- Resistant varieties show good resistance
- Sett treatment significantly reduces disease incidence
- Proper drainage reduces disease pressure

## Cost Estimate
- Sett treatment: ₹200-400/acre
- Fungicide spray: ₹600-1200/acre
- Labor: ₹250-350/acre
- Total: ₹1050-1950/acre for preventive measures
- No effective treatment after infection

## Prevention Tips
- Use certified disease-free setts
- Treat setts with hot water or fungicides
- Plant red rot-resistant varieties
- Ensure proper field drainage
- Avoid waterlogging conditions
- Remove and destroy infected stools immediately
- Implement crop rotation practices
- Maintain balanced fertilization
- Control insect pests that create wounds
