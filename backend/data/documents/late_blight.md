# Late Blight (Phytophthora infestans)

## Overview
Late blight is a devastating fungal disease affecting potato and tomato plants, caused by *Phytophthora infestans*. It's historically significant for causing the Irish Potato Famine and remains a major threat to potato production worldwide.

## Symptoms
### Potato Symptoms
- Water-soaked lesions on leaves that quickly turn brown/black
- White fungal growth on leaf undersides during humid conditions
- Lesions on stems causing blackening and collapse
- Brown, firm, sunken lesions on tubers
- Copper-colored granular tissue beneath tuber skin
- Tubers may rot in storage

### Tomato Symptoms
- Water-soaked lesions on leaves, stems, and fruits
- White fungal growth on affected tissues
- Dark, leathery patches on fruits
- Rapid plant collapse in severe cases
- Fruit rot with characteristic "water-soaked" appearance

## Conditions
- Temperature 15-20°C optimal for infection
- High humidity (>90%) and leaf wetness critical
- Cool, wet weather promotes rapid spread
- Poor air circulation increases risk
- Overhead irrigation spreads spores
- Infected seed tubers and volunteer plants
- Spores survive in tubers and plant debris

## Treatment
### Chemical Control
- Chlorothalonil: 2-3g/L, protective sprays every 7-10 days
- Copper-based fungicides: 2-3g/L, preventive applications
- Mancozeb 75% WP: 2g/L for preventive sprays
- Azoxystrobin: 1-1.5mL/L, systemic action
- Cymoxanil + Mancozeb: 2g/L, curative action
- Fentin hydroxide: 1.5g/L for severe infections

### Cultural Control
- Plant certified disease-free seed tubers
- Avoid planting in fields with history of late blight
- Improve air circulation through proper spacing
- Avoid overhead irrigation, use drip irrigation
- Remove infected plants immediately
- Destroy volunteer potato and tomato plants
- Practice crop rotation (3-4 years away from solanaceous crops)
- Ensure proper drainage in fields

### Expected Recovery
- 7-14 days after chemical treatment if caught early
- 40-60% yield recovery if treated promptly
- Prevention critical - late blight spreads rapidly
- Severe infections may cause total crop loss
- Early detection and immediate action essential

## Cost Estimate
- Fungicide spray: ₹800-1500/acre
- Labor: ₹250-350/acre
- Total: ₹1050-1850/acre per application
- Multiple applications (3-4) typically needed: ₹3150-7400/acre total

## Prevention Tips
- Start preventive sprays before disease symptoms appear
- Monitor fields daily during cool, wet periods
- Use resistant varieties when available
- Avoid planting near infected fields
- Remove and destroy infected plant material immediately
- Disinfect tools and equipment between fields
- Store potatoes in cool, dry conditions
- Avoid working with plants when wet
