# Leaf Blast (Pyricularia oryzae)

## Overview
Leaf blast is a fungal disease affecting rice, wheat, and other cereals. Caused by *Pyricularia oryzae*, it causes significant yield loss.

## Symptoms
- Brown to gray lesions on leaves
- Diamond-shaped spots with darker borders
- White center with brown ring
- Lesions coalesce causing leaf death
- Can affect stems (neck blast) and panicles

## Conditions
- High humidity (>90%)
- Temperature 20-28°C optimal
- Poor nitrogen nutrition increases severity
- Dense planting increases spread

## Treatment
### Chemical Control
- Carbendazim 50% WP: 1g/L, spray every 10 days
- Propiconazole 25% EC: 0.5 mL/L
- Mancozeb 75% WP: 2g/L for preventive sprays

### Cultural Control
- Use resistant varieties
- Improve drainage
- Balanced fertilization
- Reduce planting density
- Remove infected plant debris

### Expected Recovery
- 7-14 days after chemical treatment
- 50-70% yield recovery if caught early
- Prevention more effective than cure

## Cost Estimate
- Fungicide spray: ₹500-1000/acre
- Labor: ₹200-300/acre
- Total: ₹700-1300/acre
