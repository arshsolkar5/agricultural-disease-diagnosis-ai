# Citrus Canker (Xanthomonas axonopodis pv. citri)

## Overview
Citrus canker is a bacterial disease affecting citrus plants, caused by *Xanthomonas axonopodis* pv. *citri*. It's a highly contagious disease causing significant yield loss through fruit blemishing, premature fruit drop, and tree weakening.

## Symptoms
### Leaf Symptoms
- Raised, corky lesions on leaves
- Lesions are water-soaked initially, then become corky
- Yellow halos around lesions
- Lesions may coalesce in severe cases
- Premature defoliation in heavy infections

### Fruit Symptoms
- Raised, corky lesions on fruit surface
- Lesions may have water-soaked margins
- Fruit distortion and reduced marketability
- Premature fruit drop in severe cases
- Reduced fruit quality and yield

### Stem Symptoms
- Corky lesions on twigs and branches
- Stem cankers in severe infections
- Dieback of affected branches
- Reduced tree vigor

## Conditions
- Temperature 20-30°C optimal for infection
- High humidity and rainfall critical for spread
- Warm, wet weather promotes rapid infection
- Wind and rain spread bacteria over long distances
- Human activity (tools, equipment) spreads disease
- Infected nursery stock spreads disease
- Leaf wetness required for infection
- All citrus species are susceptible

## Treatment
### Chemical Control
- Copper-based bactericides: 2-3g/L, preventive sprays
- Streptomycin sulfate: 1g/L for limited applications
- Copper oxychloride: 3g/L for preventive sprays
- Mancozeb + Copper: 2g/L for protective sprays
- No curative treatments available
- Prevention through copper sprays only

### Cultural Control
- Use certified disease-free nursery stock
- Implement strict quarantine measures
- Remove and destroy infected trees immediately
- Disinfect tools and equipment regularly
- Avoid working with trees when wet
- Control windbreaks to reduce spread
- Maintain proper orchard sanitation
- Avoid overhead irrigation when possible

### Expected Recovery
- No recovery once infected
- Prevention critical - no effective cure
- Infected trees must be removed to prevent spread
- Copper sprays protect healthy tissue only
- Early detection allows removal of individual trees
- Quarantine measures essential for control

## Cost Estimate
- Copper spray: ₹600-1200/acre
- Labor: ₹300-400/acre
- Total: ₹900-1600/acre per application
- Multiple applications (6-8) typically needed: ₹5400-12800/acre total
- Tree removal costs: ₹500-1000 per tree

## Prevention Tips
- Use certified disease-free nursery stock
- Implement strict quarantine for new plantings
- Apply preventive copper sprays regularly
- Disinfect tools between trees and orchards
- Remove and destroy infected trees immediately
- Avoid working with trees when wet
- Control movement of people and equipment
- Monitor orchards regularly for symptoms
- Report new infections to authorities
