# Panama Disease (Fusarium oxysporum f.sp. cubense)

## Overview
Panama disease, also known as Fusarium wilt, is a devastating fungal disease affecting banana plants, caused by *Fusarium oxysporum* f.sp. *cubense*. It's a soil-borne pathogen that causes vascular wilting and has destroyed banana plantations worldwide.

## Symptoms
- Yellowing and wilting of older leaves
- Wilting progresses to younger leaves
- Leaf sheaths split near pseudostem base
- Vascular tissue shows reddish-brown discoloration
- Internal pseudostem discoloration
- Stunted growth and poor fruit development
- Plant death in susceptible varieties
- Fruit bunches may fail to develop properly

## Conditions
- Temperature 25-30°C optimal for infection
- Soil pH 5-6 favors disease development
- Poor drainage increases susceptibility
- Soil-borne pathogen persists for decades
- Infected planting material spreads disease
- Water and soil movement spreads inoculum
- Warm, moist conditions promote infection
- Susceptible varieties (Cavendish) highly affected

## Treatment
### Chemical Control
- No effective chemical treatments available
- Soil fumigation limited effectiveness
- Carbendazim: limited soil drench applications
- Biocontrol agents: Trichoderma species
- No foliar fungicides effective for wilt
- Prevention through clean planting material only

### Cultural Control
- Plant resistant varieties (e.g., GCTCV, FHIA)
- Use certified disease-free tissue culture plants
- Strict quarantine to prevent spread
- Avoid infested fields for new plantings
- Improve soil drainage
- Remove and destroy infected plants immediately
- Sterilize tools and equipment
- Implement fallow periods in infested areas

### Expected Recovery
- No recovery once infected
- Prevention critical - no effective cure
- Resistant varieties can be grown in infested soil
- Soil solarization may reduce inoculum
- Biocontrol agents show some promise
- Complete plant removal necessary to prevent spread

## Cost Estimate
- Resistant variety planting material: ₹3000-5000/acre
- Soil solarization: ₹2000-3000/acre
- Biocontrol applications: ₹500-1000/acre
- Total: ₹5500-9000/acre for establishment
- No treatment costs after infection - plants must be removed

## Prevention Tips
- Use certified disease-free tissue culture plants
- Plant resistant varieties in high-risk areas
- Implement strict quarantine measures
- Avoid moving soil from infested areas
- Sterilize tools between fields
- Remove and destroy infected plants immediately
- Improve soil drainage
- Monitor fields regularly for early symptoms
- Avoid planting in known infested areas
