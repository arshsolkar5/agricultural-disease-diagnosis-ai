# Sheath Blight (Rhizoctonia solani)

## Overview
Fungal disease causing sheaths rot in rice. Caused by *Rhizoctonia solani*, spreads rapidly in warm, humid conditions.

## Symptoms
- Oval lesions on leaf sheaths
- Brown with grayish center
- Irregular borders
- Lesions merge causing sheath collapse
- Panicle infection causes grain discoloration

## Conditions
- Temperature 25-30°C optimal
- High humidity and poor drainage
- Nitrogen excess promotes disease
- Spreads through water splash

## Treatment
### Chemical Control
- Tricyclazole 75% WP: 1g/L
- Hexaconazole 5% SC: 1.5 mL/L
- Iprodione 50% WP: 1.5g/L

### Cultural Control
- Avoid excess nitrogen
- Improve water management
- Remove plant debris
- Crop rotation (2-3 years)
- Use disease-free seeds

### Expected Recovery
- 10-15 days post-treatment
- 60-80% yield recovery
- Prevention critical (cultural methods)

## Cost Estimate
- Fungicide: ₹600-1200/acre
- Total: ₹800-1500/acre
