# Chickpea Wilt (Fusarium oxysporum f.sp. ciceris)

## Overview
Chickpea wilt is a fungal disease affecting chickpea plants, caused by *Fusarium oxysporum* f.sp. *ciceris*. It's a soil-borne pathogen that causes vascular wilting and significant yield loss, particularly in susceptible varieties.

## Symptoms
- Yellowing and wilting of lower leaves
- Wilting progresses upward through plant
- Vascular tissue turns brown/discolored
- Stunted growth and poor pod development
- Plant death in severe cases
- Reduced seed yield and quality
- Symptoms often appear during flowering/pod filling
- Wilting may be temporary initially, becoming permanent

## Conditions
- Temperature 20-30°C optimal for infection
- Soil moisture stress increases susceptibility
- Poor drainage and waterlogging
- Soil pH 6.5-7.5 favors disease
- Continuous chickpea cultivation increases risk
- Infected seed and soil serve as inoculum
- High nitrogen fertilization increases susceptibility
- Warm, dry conditions after infection promote wilting

## Treatment
### Chemical Control
- Carbendazim 50% WP: 2g/L seed treatment
- Trichoderma viride: 10g/kg seed treatment
- Thiophanate-methyl: 2g/kg seed treatment
- Soil drench with Carbendazim: 1g/L
- Biocontrol agents: Pseudomonas fluorescens seed treatment
- No effective foliar fungicides for wilt control

### Cultural Control
- Plant resistant varieties when available
- Use certified disease-free seeds
- Practice crop rotation (3-4 years away from chickpea)
- Avoid fields with history of wilt
- Improve soil drainage
- Maintain balanced fertilization
- Avoid excessive nitrogen
- Use deep plowing to reduce soil inoculum
- Solarization of soil in hot climates

### Expected Recovery
- Limited recovery once symptoms appear
- Prevention critical - no effective cure
- Early planting can escape peak infection
- Resistant varieties show good recovery
- Soil biocontrol can reduce disease incidence
- Crop rotation significantly reduces risk

## Cost Estimate
- Seed treatment: ₹100-200/acre
- Soil treatment: ₹300-500/acre
- Total: ₹400-700/acre for preventive measures
- No effective treatment costs after infection

## Prevention Tips
- Use certified wilt-resistant varieties
- Treat seeds with fungicides/biocontrol agents
- Implement long-term crop rotation (3-4 years)
- Avoid planting in wilt-infested fields
- Maintain proper soil drainage
- Balance fertilization, avoid excess nitrogen
- Use deep plowing to reduce soil inoculum
- Implement soil solarization in hot regions
