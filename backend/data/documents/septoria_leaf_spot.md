# Septoria Leaf Spot (Septoria lycopersici)

## Overview
Septoria leaf spot is a fungal disease affecting tomato plants, caused by *Septoria lycopersici*. It's one of the most common tomato diseases, causing significant yield loss through leaf damage and defoliation.

## Symptoms
- Small, circular water-soaked spots on leaves (1-3mm diameter)
- Spots develop dark brown borders with gray centers
- Numerous spots on a single leaf, giving a speckled appearance
- Yellowing (chlorosis) around spots
- Spots primarily on lower leaves initially
- In severe cases, leaves turn yellow and drop
- Stem lesions are rare but can occur
- Fruit is usually not affected

## Conditions
- Temperature 20-25°C optimal for infection
- High humidity (>90%) and leaf wetness required
- Warm, wet weather promotes rapid spread
- Poor air circulation increases risk
- Overcrowding and dense planting
- Overhead irrigation spreads spores
- Spores survive in plant debris and soil

## Treatment
### Chemical Control
- Chlorothalonil: 2-3g/L, spray every 7-10 days
- Copper-based fungicides: 2-3g/L, preventive sprays
- Mancozeb 75% WP: 2g/L for preventive sprays
- Azoxystrobin: 1-1.5mL/L, systemic action
- Iprodione 50% WP: 1.5g/L for curative action
- Myclobutanil: 1-2mL/L, systemic fungicide

### Cultural Control
- Remove infected leaves immediately
- Improve air circulation through proper spacing
- Avoid overhead irrigation, use drip irrigation
- Stake plants to improve air flow
- Mulch to reduce soil splash
- Crop rotation (3-4 years away from solanaceous crops)
- Remove and destroy plant debris at season end
- Use resistant varieties when available
- Ensure proper fertilization
- Disinfect tools between plants

### Expected Recovery
- 7-14 days after chemical treatment if caught early
- 50-70% yield recovery if treated promptly
- Prevention more effective than cure
- Severe infections may cause permanent yield loss
- Early detection and treatment critical

## Cost Estimate
- Fungicide spray: ₹400-800/acre
- Labor: ₹150-250/acre
- Total: ₹550-1050/acre per application
- Multiple applications (2-3) typically needed: ₹1100-3150/acre total

## Prevention Tips
- Start preventive sprays before symptoms appear
- Monitor plants regularly, especially after rainy periods
- Maintain plant vigor through proper nutrition
- Remove lower leaves to improve air circulation
- Disinfect tools between plants
- Use disease-free seeds and transplants
- Avoid working with plants when wet
- Keep garden area free of weeds and debris
