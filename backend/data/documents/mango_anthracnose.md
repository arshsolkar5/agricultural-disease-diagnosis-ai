# Mango Anthracnose (Colletotrichum gloeosporioides)

## Overview
Mango anthracnose is a fungal disease affecting mango trees, caused by *Colletotrichum gloeosporioides*. It's the most common and destructive mango disease, affecting flowers, leaves, and fruits, causing significant yield loss and fruit quality reduction.

## Symptoms
### Flower Symptoms
- Blackening and blighting of inflorescences
- Flower drop and reduced fruit set
- Dark lesions on flower panicles
- Complete flower blight in severe cases

### Leaf Symptoms
- Dark, irregular lesions on leaves
- Lesions may have lighter centers
- Premature leaf drop in severe infections
- Shot-hole appearance as lesions expand

### Fruit Symptoms
- Dark, sunken lesions on fruits
- Lesions expand with pinkish spore masses
- Fruit cracking and deformation
- Post-harvest rot during storage
- Reduced marketability and shelf life

## Conditions
- Temperature 25-30°C optimal for infection
- High humidity (>90%) and rainfall critical
- Warm, wet weather promotes rapid spread
- Overhead irrigation increases risk
- Poor air circulation in dense canopy
- Infected plant debris serves as inoculum
- Blossom infection leads to fruit infection
- Post-harvest infection during storage

## Treatment
### Chemical Control
- Carbendazim 50% WP: 1g/L, spray every 10-12 days
- Propiconazole 25% EC: 0.5 mL/L
- Azoxystrobin: 1-1.5 mL/L, preventive sprays
- Mancozeb 75% WP: 2g/L for preventive applications
- Thiophanate-methyl: 1g/L for curative action
- Copper oxychloride: 3g/L for preventive sprays

### Cultural Control
- Plant resistant varieties when available
- Prune trees to improve air circulation
- Remove and destroy infected plant debris
- Avoid overhead irrigation when possible
- Ensure proper drainage in orchard
- Apply balanced fertilization
- Use post-harvest fungicide treatments
- Implement proper orchard sanitation

### Expected Recovery
- 7-14 days after chemical treatment if caught early
- 50-70% yield recovery if treated promptly
- Prevention critical due to flower susceptibility
- Severe infections may cause permanent yield loss
- Post-harvest treatments reduce storage losses

## Cost Estimate
- Fungicide spray: ₹800-1500/acre
- Labor: ₹300-400/acre
- Total: ₹1100-1900/acre per application
- Multiple applications (4-6) typically needed: ₹4400-11400/acre total

## Prevention Tips
- Start preventive sprays before flowering
- Continue sprays through fruit development
- Apply post-harvest fungicide treatments
- Prune trees to improve air circulation
- Remove and destroy infected material
- Avoid overhead irrigation during flowering
- Maintain orchard sanitation
- Monitor fields regularly during wet periods
